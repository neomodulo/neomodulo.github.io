TI-83 Series

TI-83

The TI-83 was released in 1996 as a follow-up to the TI-82. It introduced a slew of new graphing modes, having polar, parametric, and sequence graphing, and a much improved TI-Basic.

TI-83 Plus

In 1999, the TI-83 Plus was introduced, adding 160KB of rewritable flash memory, (dubbed "Archive Memory"), and provided less RAM due to address space limitations. This is the best selling 83 model, and the last still being sold in the lineup.

View the entry for this calculator on our buyer's guide.

TI-83 Plus Silver Edition

Finally, in 2001, the TI-83 Plus Silver Edition was released, and quickly discontinued. It featured a 3x boost in clock speed, almost 10x the amount of flash memory, and over 4x more RAM. However, most of the extra RAM was not available to the user. It also featured a silver transluscent case with glitter.
